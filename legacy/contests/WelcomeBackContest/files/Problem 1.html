Welcome back to the World of Math!

Your brother George is struggling with Math again. He is learning about medians, modes and averages. On a bonus problem on his homework, he is given a grid of numbers, and asked to calculate the median of the medians, the mode of the modes, and the average of the averages.


Input Specifications

The first line will contain N (N < 250, N will be odd).
The next N lines will contain N integers each, space-separated.


Output Specifications

The output will contain 3 integers on one line, the median of the medians, the mode of the modes, and the average of the averages.
The median is the middle number of a sequence when sorted in increasing order.
The mode is the most common element of a sequence, with ties broken by whichever element is larger.
The average is the sum of the numbers in the sequence divided by the length of the sequence.
Round the final average to 2 decimal places. If the final average is precise to fewer than 2 digits, pad on extra zeroes so that the final output has a decimal point followed by exactly two digits.


Subtasks

For 20% of the available 100 marks, N < 10.
For an additional 20% of the available 100 marks, N < 100.


Sample Input

3
1 2 2
4 3 4
7 5 6

Sample Output

4 7 3.78
