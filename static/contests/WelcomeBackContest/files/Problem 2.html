Welcome to my Hotel!

Joe manages an apartment in KW. Being a businessman, heâ€™d like to get the most amount of people in his hotel, so he can make a lot of money! Unfortunately, some of the customers have special requests. Each customer has a room size request, the size of the room (from 1 to S inclusive) that they want in the hotel. Joe would like to keep them all happy, so giving a customer a room bigger than they asked for is fine. However, Joe cannot give them a smaller room, so at some point he might run out of rooms. Tell Joe how many people he can service.


Input Specifications

The first line will contain three integers, N(N<=100 000), M(M<=100 000) and S(S<=100 000) the number of people, the numbers of rooms and the maximum room size.
The second line will contain N integers, the size requests of the N people.
The third line will contain M integers, the sizes of all the rooms.


Output Specifications

Output the number of people that can be serviced a room at least as large as they requested.


Subtasks

For 20% of the available 100 marks, N,M,S <= 100
For another 20% of the available 100 marks, S<=10
For another 20% of the available 100 marks, N,M <= 1000
For another 20% of the available 100 marks, S <= 100


Sample Input

4 3 3
1 2 1 3
1 1 1

Sample Output

2
